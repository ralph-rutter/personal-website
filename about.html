Ralph Rutter

I am a vibrant and conscientious mathematician with an MMath, 1st class honours, from the University of Surrey. My love of mathematics and language is driven by a desire for completeness, enlightenment, clarity, and precision; not to mention an appreciation of their intricate beauty.

I am an eloquent communicator, proficient at explaining abstract and technical concepts to the unacquainted, and experienced in collaborating within a large team. I believe in things being done the right way, without cutting corners; as a result I operate with thoroughness, patience and attention to detail. When making decisions, positivity, respect and a strong code of ethics are priorities for me; this is reflected in my words and actions.

Central to maths is the problem solving process: abstract the brief to shed unnecessary information, systematise what remains, then formulate it symbolically so that mathematical techniques can be applied. Engaging in this practice has given me robust tools that can be used in any of a myriad of applications involving discerning patterns, logical reasoning, understanding interrelated processes, modelling behaviour or rigorous constraints.

Ultimately, I would like to make use of the technical knowledge and skills attained in my degree, advising and working alongside engineers, scientists, analysts and developers, with the goal of expanding knowledge and creating solutions. Fields of interest include:

  • Environmentalism
  • Humanitarianism
  • Engineering
  • Academic research
  • Technology
  • Urban planning
  • Modelling
  • Software and web development
  • Cyber security
  • Communications
